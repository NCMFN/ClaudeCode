Research Proposal Presentation
Dietary Exposure Risk:  Predicting Blood Microplastic Loads
A Data-Driven ML Approach to Bioaccumulation Prediction
Expert Academic Researcher & Data Scientist Perspective

Microplastics (MPs) are ubiquitous environmental contaminants detected in human lungs, liver, and placenta.
Recent studies (2022-2024) confirm MP presence in the human bloodstream.
Urgency: Need for individual risk profiling based on daily dietary and lifestyle factors.
Background & Motivation

Quantification Gap: High variability in MP detection results; lack of direct linkage to dietary source metrics.
Health Risk: Chronic bioaccumulation is linked to inflammation and oxidative stress, yet exposure levels are unmonitored.
Accessibility: Current blood testing for MPs is invasive, expensive, and unavailable to the general public.
Problem Statement

ML Task
Build a robust multi-class classifier to predict biological MP risk levels.
Key Pathways
Map high-risk "entry routes" like plastic-packaged foods and seafood.
Scalability
Create a non-invasive screening tool based on dietary intake metadata.
Research Objectives

Hypothesis 1
Individuals consuming >4 items of plastic-packaged processed food daily show a 70% higher probability of "High Risk" classification.
Hypothesis 2
Fresh, plant-based diets serve as a predictive indicator for "Negligible" or "Low" MP load, regardless of urban proximity.
Research Hypotheses

 "For the first time, we have shown that microplastics are in human blood. This study suggests that we are ingesting plastic daily through various environmental vectors."
— Leslie et al. (2022), Environment International
Gap: Existing studies focus on environmental detection; this research moves toward behavioral prediction.
